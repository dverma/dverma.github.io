Exercise Progression Overview

Week 1: Foundation

Focus on proper form and establish baseline. Use lighter weights and focus on mind-muscle connection.

Week 2: Build

Increase repetitions while maintaining same weights. Focus on mind-muscle connection. Shorten rest periods slightly.

Week 3: Progress

Introduce slight weight increases where appropriate. Add exercise variations (e.g., single-leg glute bridges). Further reduce rest times.

Week 4: Intensity

Increase sets for key exercises. Focus on controlled eccentric (lowering) phase. Extend cardio intervals to 12 minutes.

Week 5: Challenge

Introduce more challenging variations. Continue progressive overload. Increase workout density.

Week 6: Peak

Maximize effort with good form. Challenge yourself with manageable weight increases. Extend cardio intervals to 15 minutes.

Shoulder Safety Guidelines

  • Always perform the complete warm-up routine, especially the rotator cuff activation exercises
  • If you feel unusual shoulder pain (not normal muscle fatigue), stop the exercise immediately
  • Prioritize proper form over increasing weight
  • Avoid extreme overhead movements and positions that place your shoulder at risk
  • For pressing movements, keep elbows slightly tucked rather than flared out
  • Consider reducing range of motion on any exercise that causes shoulder discomfort

Warm-Up Protocol (5-7 minutes before each workout)

1. Light Cardio (3-5 minutes)

Walking or jogging on treadmill at a comfortable pace

2. Dynamic Mobility Work

  • Arm circles: 10 forward, 10 backward (small, controlled movements)
  • Shoulder blade squeezes: 10 repetitions
  • Hip circles: 5 each direction
  • Bodyweight squats: 10 repetitions
  • Gentle torso rotations: 5 each side

3. Rotator Cuff Activation with Resistance Band

  • External rotation: 10 repetitions each arm
  • Internal rotation: 10 repetitions each arm
  • Band pull-aparts: 10 repetitions

Cool-Down Protocol (5 minutes after each workout)

1. Light Cardio (1-2 minutes)

Walking at slow pace to gradually lower heart rate

2. Static Stretching (Hold each for 20-30 seconds)

  • Hamstring stretch
  • Quad stretch
  • Gentle chest stretch
  • Upper back/trapezius stretch
  • Gentle shoulder stretch (avoiding extreme positions)

3. Deep Breathing

5 deep breaths, focusing on complete exhalation
